<p><p>Quartz crucibles, used primarily in the semiconductor and solar industries, can be categorized into opaque and transparent types based on their optical properties. Opaque quartz crucibles are designed to hold molten materials at high temperatures while preventing light penetration, making them ideal for applications requiring thermal insulation. Transparent quartz crucibles, on the other hand, allow for light transmission, enabling monitoring of material behavior during processes like crystal growth. Each type serves distinct functions, catering to varying industry needs and specific operational requirements.</p></p>
